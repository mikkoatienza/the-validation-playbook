---
title: "Stage 2: Diagnose"
description: "Prove the problem is urgent and not already owned. Score the pain with evidence, not enthusiasm."
---

Stage 1 gave you candidates. Stage 2 chooses the one worth the build.  
This is where evidence gets sharp.  
You will test the riskiest assumption, score the pain, and scan alternatives.

## Stage Promise
By the end of Stage 2, you will have one prioritized problem with a clear
risk map, a strong problem score, and a documented alternatives scan.

If you cannot name the riskiest assumption, you may not be ready for the offer.

This is a decision stage. You are not proving that your idea is possible. You
are proving that it is worth the cost of building.

## What This Stage Produces
Stage 2 turns a shortlist into a single, defensible problem. You will build
three artifacts that make the choice clear:

- **Risk Map** (top assumptions ranked by evidence)
- **Problem Scorecard** (pain frequency, intensity, spend, workarounds)
- **Alternatives Grid** (competitors, substitutes, DIY)

These are the proof points that justify moving into Stage 3.

Your goal is not to be right once. Your goal is to be right enough to bet
the next 90 days on this problem.

## Stage Rules (Keep It Honest)
This stage works only if you follow a few rules:

- Test the riskiest assumption first, not the easiest.
- Evidence beats optimism. A weak signal is still a signal.
- Avoid ignoring DIY workarounds. They are your real competition.
- If the score is low, narrow the problem or walk away.

Depth beats breadth here. One strong problem beats three vague ones.

## How the Modules Fit
This stage has three modules, each with a specific activity:

1. **Find the one assumption that could kill the idea** (Activity: Risk Map)  
   You surface the risk that can erase months of work.

2. **Prove the pain is frequent and costly** (Activity: Problem Proof)  
   You score the pain so you stop solving mild annoyances.

3. **See what people use instead** (Activity: Alternatives Scan)  
   You map the battlefield, including competitors and DIY.

Each module narrows the problem. If Module 1 is weak, the score becomes shaky.
If Module 2 is soft, the scan loses value. Do them in order.

## What to Ignore for Now
Stage 2 is not the time for:

- Polishing the product or branding
- Perfecting the pitch deck
- Scaling acquisition channels
- Hiring or fundraising plans

Those moves depend on proof you do not yet have.

## Common Traps (Avoid Them Early)
Most founders get stuck in one of these:

- Testing what is easy instead of what is risky
- Mistaking enthusiasm for evidence
- Ignoring substitutes because they look informal
- Believing “no competitors” means safety
- Falling in love with a clever solution

If you recognize one, stop and reset. The goal is not speed. The goal is
accuracy.

## Pace and Volume
Speed matters here, but only if you keep the evidence clean. A good rhythm is:

- One risk test in the next two weeks
- One full scorecard on your top problem
- One alternatives scan with at least 3 direct and 3 substitute options

Avoid over‑polishing. Your job is to surface the risk, not to perfect the story.

## Evidence Standard (What Counts)
You do not need a perfect dataset, but you do need real signals:

- The riskiest assumption can be tested this month
- The problem score is high with evidence
- Alternatives leave a clear gap or underserved segment

If you do not yet have those signals, pause and fix the gap.

## Evidence Depth (Strong vs. Weak)
Not all evidence is equal. Use this simple lens:

- **Weak**: opinions, likes, polite interest
- **Medium**: past actions, real workarounds, specific costs
- **Strong**: buyers spending time or money to fix the pain

Stage 2 is about moving from weak to strong. If you are stuck in weak signals,
avoid moving on.

## What Success Feels Like
By the end of this stage, the problem should feel narrower and more urgent.
You should be able to say, “This is the risk, this is the score, and this is
the gap.” If you cannot, the problem is not ready.

Stage 2 is your filter. It protects you from building something the market
already has or does not need.

## Handoff to Module 1
Start with the risk. Find the assumption that can erase months of work and
put it on paper. Module 1 begins with the Risk Map.

One last reminder: depth first, always.
