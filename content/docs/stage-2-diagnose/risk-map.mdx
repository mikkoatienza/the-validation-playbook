## The Assumption Stack

An assumption is a belief about the world that must be true for your idea to work. When you stack ten assumptions together, your idea becomes a tower. The question is which block, if pulled, collapses the whole thing.

<Callout type="warn" title="The core rule">
One wrong assumption can erase months of work. Test the riskiest one first. If you test the easy assumption, you only feel progress. If you test the riskiest one, you get truth.
</Callout>

Webvan built massive delivery infrastructure on the assumption that enough people would switch to online grocery at scale. Demand did not match the cost structure. The hidden assumption was not "people want groceries delivered" -- it was "enough people will change habits fast enough to make this model work." Wrong assumption, wrong order, real collapse.
