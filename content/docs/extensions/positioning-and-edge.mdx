## Section A: Positioning After Proof

Positioning comes after proof, not before it. Your evidence tells you where you can win.

<div className="mt-4 rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-5" style={{backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--fd-muted) / 0.4)'}}>
  <p className="text-[10px] font-semibold uppercase tracking-wider text-accent-warm">The simplest definition</p>
  <p className="mt-3 font-[family-name:var(--font-display)] text-xl text-fd-foreground">Positioning is the decision about <strong>who it is for</strong>, <strong>what it replaces</strong>, and <strong>why it is the better choice</strong>.</p>
  <p className="mt-3 text-xs italic text-fd-muted-foreground">If you can only describe what you built, you do not have positioning yet. If you can name who it is not for, you are getting close.</p>
</div>

Every strong position is a tradeoff. You choose a buyer and let another go. You choose a pain to solve and ignore others. If you refuse the tradeoff, you may be positioned for nobody.

If any rung is missing, the position is still a draft. Cirque du Soleil did not make a "better circus" -- they changed who it was for, how it was priced, and why it was worth it. Blue oceans are made, not found.

<Callout type="warn" title="Positioning guardrails">
One buyer segment, not three. One urgent pain, not a list. One alternative you replace most often. One proof point you can show right now. If any guardrail breaks, your position will blur.

## Section B: Differentiation That Holds

Differentiation is not being different. It is being the better choice in a specific moment for a specific buyer.

<div className="mt-4 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4 transition-all hover:border-accent-warm/30">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-2"><span className="flex h-7 w-7 items-center justify-center rounded-lg text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">T</span><span className="text-sm font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Time saved</span></div>
    <p className="mt-1.5 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">You save time in a measurable way</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4 transition-all hover:border-accent-warm/30">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-2"><span className="flex h-7 w-7 items-center justify-center rounded-lg text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">R</span><span className="text-sm font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Risk removed</span></div>
    <p className="mt-1.5 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">You remove a risk buyers already fear</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4 transition-all hover:border-accent-warm/30">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-2"><span className="flex h-7 w-7 items-center justify-center rounded-lg text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">A</span><span className="text-sm font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Access simplified</span></div>
    <p className="mt-1.5 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">You make access simpler or more reliable</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4 transition-all hover:border-accent-warm/30">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-2"><span className="flex h-7 w-7 items-center justify-center rounded-lg text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">C</span><span className="text-sm font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Cost changed</span></div>
    <p className="mt-1.5 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">You change the total cost, not just the sticker price</p>
  </div>
</div>

Buyers switch because of deadlines, cost spikes, new requirements, or repeated failures -- not because of features. Dollar Shave Club's wedge was not "the best razor" but a convenient way to avoid overpriced blades. The edge was price + convenience + a clear buyer story.

## Section C: Moat Seeds
