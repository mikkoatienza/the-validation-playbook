## Section B: Retention and the Core Loop

Retention is the heartbeat of momentum. If buyers do not return, growth just creates a bigger leak.

<div className="mt-4 rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-5" style={{backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--fd-muted) / 0.4)'}}>
  <p className="text-[10px] font-semibold uppercase tracking-wider text-accent-warm">The core loop</p>
  <div className="mt-4 flex flex-col gap-2">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-4 rounded-lg border border-fd-border bg-fd-background p-3"><span className="flex h-8 w-8 shrink-0 items-center justify-center rounded-full text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">T</span><div><p className="text-xs font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Trigger</p><p className="text-[10px] text-fd-muted-foreground">A real reason to act (calendar, risk, threshold)</p></div></div>
    <div className="flex items-center gap-4 rounded-lg border border-fd-border bg-fd-background p-3"><span className="flex h-8 w-8 shrink-0 items-center justify-center rounded-full text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">A</span><div><p className="text-xs font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Action</p><p className="text-[10px] text-fd-muted-foreground">What the user does (run the report, check the list)</p></div></div>
    <div className="flex items-center gap-4 rounded-lg border border-fd-border bg-fd-background p-3"><span className="flex h-8 w-8 shrink-0 items-center justify-center rounded-full text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">R</span><div><p className="text-xs font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Reward</p><p className="text-[10px] text-fd-muted-foreground">What makes it feel worth it (risk avoided, time saved)</p></div></div>
    <div className="flex items-center gap-4 rounded-lg border border-fd-border bg-fd-background p-3"><span className="flex h-8 w-8 shrink-0 items-center justify-center rounded-full text-xs font-bold text-white bg-accent-warm">L</span><div><p className="text-xs font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Return</p><p className="text-[10px] text-fd-muted-foreground">Why they come back (new trigger, saved state, habit)</p></div></div>
  </div>
  <p className="mt-3 text-xs italic text-fd-muted-foreground">Reminders are not triggers. If users only come back after a reminder, your loop is still weak.</p>
</div>

<div className="mt-4 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-2">
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4">
    <p className="text-xs font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Loop breaks when:</p>
    <div className="mt-2 space-y-1 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">
      <p>Trigger is weak or inconsistent</p>
      <p>Action is too hard or slow</p>
      <p>Reward is unclear or delayed</p>
      <p>Return is optional, not needed</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4">
    <p className="text-xs font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Retention three-step:</p>
    <div className="mt-2 space-y-1 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">
      <p className="flex items-start gap-2"><span className="text-accent-warm">1.</span> Activation: make first value obvious</p>
      <p className="flex items-start gap-2"><span className="text-accent-warm">2.</span> Habit: make the loop easy to repeat</p>
      <p className="flex items-start gap-2"><span className="text-accent-warm">3.</span> Reactivation: bring people back after a break</p>
    </div>
    <p className="mt-2 text-[10px] italic text-fd-muted-foreground">Work in order. Spike without habit = noise.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Section C: Growth Loops

Growth comes from loops, not campaigns. If retention is weak, growth is just expensive churn.

<Callout type="warn" title="The order matters">
Retention first, then growth. If you stack growth before retention is stable, you magnify churn. A campaign spike that ends when spend stops is not a loop.
</Callout>
