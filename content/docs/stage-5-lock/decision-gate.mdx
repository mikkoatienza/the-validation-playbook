You have evidence now. The only thing left is the decision. Most founders avoid this moment because it feels final. It is not. It is simply honest.

<Callout type="idea" title="Output for this module">
- A documented go / pivot / stop statement
- A decision grid with evidence notes
- A short commitment plan or stop rationale
</Callout>

## The Three Paths

    <p className="mt-2 text-[10px] italic" style={{color: 'hsl(var(--stage-5))'}}>Focus the build around the proven loop.</p>

    <p className="mt-2 text-[10px] italic text-fd-muted-foreground">A new outcome and a fresh test, not a rename.</p>

    <p className="mt-1 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">Repeat use is near zero after focused tests. Pricing fails even after a clean offer.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-[10px] italic text-red-500">Close the loop and free your time.</p>
  </div>

<Callout type="warn" title="Delay is not a decision">
Delay feels safe, but it steals momentum. If you avoid the gate, the market will decide for you. Give yourself a 7-10 day window to decide. If it drags, the decision is turning into avoidance. Indecision is often slow failure.
</Callout>

Mailchimp started as a side project inside a web design agency. Over time, the email tool grew while the agency stalled. The founders chose the path with proof -- they cut the distraction to commit to the product with pull.

<Callout type="error" title="Common traps">
- Moving the goalposts because the answer is uncomfortable
- Calling "pivot" when you really mean "avoid stop"
- Choosing "go" because you are tired
- Ignoring a weak signal because one strong signal felt good
- Letting sunk cost speak louder than evidence
- Hiding behind "one more test" without a pass line
</Callout>
