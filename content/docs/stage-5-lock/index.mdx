---
title: "Stage 5: Lock"
description: "Commit or walk away with clarity. Review the signal, confirm founder fit, and install the rhythm."
---

This is the final gate. You have evidence. Now you decide.  
Go, pivot, or stop — and install the weekly system that keeps you honest.  
Stage 5 is where drift ends and commitment begins.

## Stage Promise
By the end of Stage 5, you should have:

- A documented go / pivot / stop decision (Decision Gate)
- A founder‑fit (founder‑problem fit) score with a mitigation plan
- A weekly evidence cadence that you can execute

If you cannot decide, the market will often decide for you.
Keep the decision small and real.

## What This Stage Produces
Stage 5 turns evidence into a decision and a system. You will build three
artifacts that lock the path:

- **Decision Gate Summary** (go / pivot / stop evidence grid)
- **Founder Fit Check** (scores + gaps + mitigation)
- **Momentum OS Plan** (weekly cadence + evidence log)

These are the foundations for real commitment.

Your goal is not to feel ready. Your goal is to decide and install a rhythm
that keeps the evidence honest.

## Stage Rules (Keep It Honest)
This stage works best when you follow a few rules:

- Evidence decides, not ego.
- A weak loop means no commitment yet.
- Founder fit matters as much as product fit.
- A system beats motivation.

This is the stage where you stop drifting.

## How the Modules Fit
This stage has three modules, each with a specific activity:

1. **Decide to commit or stop** (Activity: Decision Gate)  
   You make the go / pivot / stop call based on evidence.

2. **Decide if you are the right founder** (Activity: Founder Fit)  
   You test insight, access, and credibility.

3. **Install a weekly system that keeps you honest** (Activity: Momentum OS)  
   You schedule the evidence rhythm that protects momentum.

Each module removes drift. If Module 1 is fuzzy, Module 2 is defensive. If
Module 2 is weak, Module 3 collapses. Do them in order.

## What to Ignore for Now
Stage 5 is not the time for:

- Big hiring plans
- Fundraising roadshows
- Scaling before the loop is stable
- Long‑range roadmaps

Those moves depend on the decision you make here.

## Common Traps (Avoid Them Early)
Most founders get stuck in one of these:

- Avoiding the decision by “one more test”
- Committing without founder fit
- Saying “go” without a system
- Letting sunk cost override evidence
- Confusing momentum with motion

If you recognize one, stop and reset. The goal is not to feel good. The goal
is to be honest.

## Pace and Volume
Speed matters here, but only if the decision is real. A good rhythm is:

- One clear decision this week
- One founder‑fit check completed in a single sitting
- One Momentum OS schedule set before next Monday

Avoid over‑polishing. Your job is to decide and install the rhythm, not to
create a perfect plan.

## Evidence Standard (What Counts)
You do not need perfection, but you do need real signals:

- A decision backed by evidence
- A founder‑fit score you can defend
- A weekly cadence you can execute

If you do not have those signals, pause and fix the gap.

## Evidence Depth (Strong vs. Weak)
Not all signals are equal. Use this lens:

- **Weak**: vague intention, “maybe,” or endless delay
- **Medium**: a written decision with a thin plan
- **Strong**: a decision plus a real calendar and owners

Stage 5 is about turning a decision into action. If the signal is weak, the
system is likely to collapse.

## Reality Check (Fast)
Ask yourself:

- "Is the decision written and scheduled?"
- "Do I know who owns the weekly review?"
- "Can I execute the next sprint without new resources?"

If any answer is no, slow down and fix it. This stage is cheaper than drifting.

## What Success Feels Like
By the end of this stage, you should be able to say, “We chose a path, we can
win this fight, and we have a weekly system to stay honest.” If you cannot,
you may not be ready to commit.

Stage 5 is your lock. It turns evidence into a decision and a system.

## Handoff to Module 1
Start with the decision. If you cannot decide, nothing else will hold. Module 1
begins with the Decision Gate.

One last reminder: decisions without systems fade.
