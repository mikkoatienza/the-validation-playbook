You entered this stage with evidence. You leave it with a decision and a
system. That is the point. You are no longer waiting for clarity. You created
it.

## The Three Artifacts That Matter
If you completed Stage 5 as designed, you now have:

- A go / pivot / stop decision with evidence notes
- A founder‑fit score and mitigation plan
- A weekly evidence cadence on your calendar

That is the minimum foundation for building with confidence.

## Readiness Gate (30 Seconds)
Use this compact gate to confirm the stage is complete:

- **Decision Gate:** decision written with evidence
- **Founder Fit:** scores + mitigation plan
- **Momentum OS:** weekly sprint schedule + evidence log
- **Commitment Plan:** next 30‑day focus

If any box is empty, slow down and fill it.

## Readiness Questions (Fast)
Ask yourself:

- "Is the decision written and scheduled?"
- "Do I know who owns the weekly review?"
- "Can I execute the next sprint with what I have?"

If any answer is no, tighten the evidence before you move forward.

## If the Evidence Is Thin
Avoid forcing the commitment. Use one of these fixes:

- Run one more focused test before deciding
- Strengthen the weakest fit dimension
- Build a tighter loop before you scale
- Shorten the timebox and re‑decide

The goal is not to feel certain. The goal is to be honest.

## Module Recap (What Each One Gave You)
Stage 5 worked because each module did a specific job:

- **Decision Gate** forced a clear go / pivot / stop call.
- **Founder Fit** revealed whether you can win this fight.
- **Momentum OS** installed the rhythm that keeps you honest.

If any module feels weak, fix it before you move on. A weak decision creates a
weak business.

## Stage 5 Retrospective (Two Questions)
Write short answers before you move forward:

- What decision did you avoid the longest, and why?
- What evidence finally made it clear?

That reflection will keep you from drifting back into indecision.

## Decision Summary (One Sentence)
Write this line and keep it with your artifacts:

“We chose [go/pivot/stop] because [evidence], and we will execute [plan].”

If that line is hard to write, slow down and tighten the decision.

## Evidence Snapshot (One Paragraph)
Before you move on, write a single paragraph that captures:

- The decision and why you made it
- The weakest fit gap you still need to close
- The weekly cadence you will protect

Keep it tight. If you cannot write it in one paragraph, the evidence is still
too fuzzy. Tighten it now while the work is fresh.

## If You Chose to Stop
Write one paragraph that answers: “What would have to change for this to be
worth revisiting?” This turns a stop decision into future clarity.

## Carry‑Forward Signals
Before you enter the Extensions and Closing, underline the strongest proof:

- The decision and why you made it
- The weakest fit gap you still need to close
- The weekly cadence you will protect

Those signals should anchor what you build next. Keep them visible.

## If Two Signals Disagree
Sometimes the decision feels clear but the cadence is weak. Use this tie‑breaker:

- Trust the signal closest to behavior, not intention
- Prefer a calendar you can execute over a plan you admire
- Re‑run the weakest test with a tighter timebox

If the signals still conflict, slow down and retest. Mixed evidence is rarely
a green light.

## If You Want to Move Faster
Speed is useful only when the decision is real. Use these safe shortcuts:

- Cut one commitment instead of cutting evidence
- Shorten the timebox and re‑decide
- Narrow the plan to the single highest‑leverage move

Moving fast means doing less, not skipping the decision.

## After You Leave This Stage
You will feel pressure to sprint ahead. Resist the urge to out‑run the system.
Momentum is a weekly ritual, not a single moment of will.

If you skip this discipline, your decision may decay into drift. Stage 5 is
the cheaper place to build a real cadence.

## The Handoff to the Extensions and Closing
Extensions will help you build positioning and momentum beyond the core
validation stages. The closing will turn this into a 30‑day proof plan.

You are about to move from “prove the idea” to “build the edge.”

## Closing Line
No decision, no momentum.
