## What Fit Looks Like

Product-market fit means the product solves a real problem so well that people keep using it without persuasion. Not forever. Not perfectly. But enough that the habit starts to form.

Activation is the first use. Retention is the second use. You can fix activation with copy and onboarding. You fix retention with real value. Match your expectations to the pain cycle -- daily problems need daily returns, weekly problems need weekly returns.

## The Momentum Loop

A momentum loop is a simple cycle that turns one use into the next. You should be able to explain it in one sentence.

<Callout type="error" title="Common traps">
- Celebrating sign-ups instead of repeat use
- Confusing a one-time spike with a habit
- Ignoring churn because a few users are loud
- Measuring engagement but not completion
- Forcing referrals before the loop works
- Shipping new features to avoid fixing the loop
</Callout>
