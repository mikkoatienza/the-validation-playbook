- A short evidence note that explains why

## The Three Paths

There are only three paths. This is not a moral decision. It is an evidence decision.

<Callout type="warn" title="The one-variable rule">
Pick one variable to change in the next test. If you change two, you will not know which one helped. If you change three, you will confuse yourself.
</Callout>

Slack started as a game company. The game failed, but the team noticed their internal chat tool had pull -- people would not give up using it. They pivoted to the tool, not the game. A good pivot preserves evidence and drops the dead weight.

<Callout type="error" title="Common traps">
- Changing three variables at once
- Chasing opinions instead of signals
- Pivoting because you are bored
- Avoiding the stop decision
- Confusing "more effort" with "better evidence"
</Callout>
