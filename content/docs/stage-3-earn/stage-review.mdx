title: "Stage 3 Review"
description: "Checklist, readiness gate, and retrospective for Stage 3: Earn."
---

You started this stage with a hypothesis. You end it with proof. That is the
shift. You now know whether people will commit, at what price, and through
which channel.

## The Three Artifacts That Matter
If you completed Stage 3 as designed, you now have:

- An Offer Test Result with real commitments
- A Price Range with evidence and objections
- A Channel Hypothesis with early signal

That is the minimum foundation for Stage 4.

## Readiness Gate (30 Seconds)
Use this compact gate to decide if you should move on:

- **Offer Test:** 3+ real commitments or deposits
- **Price Check:** a workable range with buyer reactions
- **Channel Probe:** one channel with qualified conversations
- **Commitment Summary:** evidence log + objections

If any box is empty, slow down and fill it.

## Readiness Questions (Fast)
Ask yourself:

- “Would I bet the next month on this offer?”
- “Do I know the top objection and how to test it?”
- “Can I reach buyers again without a lucky spike?”

If any answer is no, tighten the evidence before you move forward.
If you cannot say yes three times, you are not ready yet.

## If the Evidence Is Thin
Avoid forcing the next stage. Use one of these fixes first:

- Tighten the offer and retest commitments
- Adjust the price ladder and rerun the range test
- Shrink the channel test to the strongest segment
- Drop the weakest channel and focus on the strongest

The goal is not to win fast. The goal is to earn proof.

## Module Recap (What Each One Gave You)
Stage 3 worked because each module did a specific job:

- **Offer Test** earned real commitments, not polite interest.
- **Price Check** proved a price range people can accept.
- **Channel Probe** showed whether you can reach buyers consistently.

If any module feels weak, fix it before you move on. A weak earn stage creates
false confidence.

## Stage 3 Retrospective (Two Questions)
Write short answers before you move forward:

- Which commitment signal felt strongest and why?
- Which objection kept showing up and why?

That reflection will make the MVP (minimum viable product) test sharper in
Stage 4.

## Decision Summary (One Sentence)
Write this line and keep it with your artifacts:

“We earn [commitment] from [buyer] because [evidence].”

If you cannot write that line, the signal is not clear yet.

## Evidence Snapshot (One Paragraph)
Before you move on, write a single paragraph that captures:

- The strongest commitment signal you earned
- The price point that held under pressure
- The channel that produced real conversations

Keep it tight. If you cannot write it in one paragraph, the evidence is still
too fuzzy. Tighten it now while the tests are fresh.

## Carry‑Forward Signals
Before you enter Stage 4, underline the strongest proof in your notes:

- The most reliable commitment signal
- The price point that held under pressure
- The channel that produced real conversations

Those signals should anchor your MVP test. Keep them visible.

## If Two Signals Disagree
Sometimes one signal is strong and another is weak. Use this tie‑breaker:

- Trust the signal closest to money or time
- Prefer repeatable commitment over one‑time spikes
- Re‑run the weakest test with a tighter pass line

If the signals still conflict, slow down and retest. Mixed evidence is rarely
a green light.

## If You Want to Move Faster
Speed is useful only when the evidence keeps up. Use these safe shortcuts:

- Drop the weakest channel and double down on the strongest
- Retest the offer with a tighter, clearer promise
- Narrow the buyer segment until commitments are consistent

Moving fast means narrowing the focus, not skipping the proof.

## After You Leave This Stage
You will feel pressure to build. Resist the urge to celebrate too early.
Stage 4 is where you test repeat use, not just first‑time enthusiasm.

If you skip this discipline, you may build a product that gets initial
interest but not return behavior. Stage 3 earned commitment. Stage 4 proves
stickiness.

## The Handoff to Stage 4: Assess
Stage 4 will test repeat use and early fit. That work only makes sense if the
Stage 3 commitment is real.

You are about to move from “Will they commit?” to “Will they come back?”

## Closing Line
If you can earn commitment, you are ready to assess fit.
