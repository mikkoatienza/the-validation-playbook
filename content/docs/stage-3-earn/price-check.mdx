If your offer earns commitment but no one will pay the price, the offer is still fiction. Price is not a number. It is proof.

## The Price Ladder

Early pricing is not precision. It is a range. Think in three bands and see where people say yes.

<div className="mt-4 flex flex-col gap-2">
  <div className="flex items-center gap-3"><div className="flex h-9 items-center rounded-md px-3 text-xs font-medium text-white/90" style={{width: '100%', backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--stage-3))'}}> High -- tests true willingness to pay</div></div>
  <div className="flex items-center gap-3"><div className="flex h-9 items-center rounded-md px-3 text-xs font-medium text-white/90" style={{width: '70%', backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--stage-3) / 0.7)'}}> Mid -- fair value for the outcome</div></div>
  <div className="flex items-center gap-3"><div className="flex h-9 items-center rounded-md px-3 text-xs font-medium text-white/90" style={{width: '45%', backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--stage-3) / 0.45)'}}> Low -- easiest to say yes to</div></div>
</div>

<p className="mt-3 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">Netflix learned this in 2011 -- pricing that feels wrong can kill demand even when the product is loved. Pricing is not just math. It is perception.</p>

## Price Anchors

Buyers never evaluate price in a vacuum. They compare it to something.

<div className="mt-4 grid gap-3 sm:grid-cols-3">
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4 transition-all hover:border-[hsl(var(--stage-3)/0.3)]">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-2"><span className="flex h-7 w-7 items-center justify-center rounded-lg text-xs font-bold text-white" style={{backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--stage-3))'}}>$</span><span className="text-sm font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Current spend</span></div>
    <p className="mt-1.5 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">What they pay today for tools, staff time, or agencies</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4 transition-all hover:border-[hsl(var(--stage-3)/0.3)]">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-2"><span className="flex h-7 w-7 items-center justify-center rounded-lg text-xs font-bold text-white" style={{backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--stage-3))'}}>0</span><span className="text-sm font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Cost of nothing</span></div>
    <p className="mt-1.5 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">Lost revenue, delays, or risk from doing nothing</p>
  </div>
  <div className="rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-4 transition-all hover:border-[hsl(var(--stage-3)/0.3)]">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-2"><span className="flex h-7 w-7 items-center justify-center rounded-lg text-xs font-bold text-white" style={{backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--stage-3))'}}>R</span><span className="text-sm font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Replacement cost</span></div>
    <p className="mt-1.5 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">New hire, consultant, or overtime to solve it</p>
  </div>
</div>

If your price is higher than every anchor, you need a stronger outcome. If lower, you may be underpricing. Your job is to make value obvious in one sentence.

## Price Range Rules

<div className="mt-4 rounded-xl border-2 border-fd-border p-5" style={{backgroundColor: 'hsl(var(--fd-muted) / 0.4)'}}>
  <div className="space-y-3 text-xs text-fd-muted-foreground">
    <p className="flex items-start gap-2"><span className="font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Only the low price works?</span> Your offer is too weak.</p>
    <p className="flex items-start gap-2"><span className="font-semibold text-fd-foreground">High price works with no resistance?</span> Your problem is very strong.</p>
    <p className="flex items-start gap-2"><span className="font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Mid price works?</span> You have a usable starting point.</p>
    <p className="flex items-start gap-2"><span className="font-semibold text-fd-foreground">Price is the only objection?</span> Shrink the offer or shorten the timeline. Do not rush to discounts.</p>
  </div>
  <p className="mt-3 text-xs italic" style={{color: 'hsl(var(--stage-3))'}}>Cheap does not equal paid. Paid equals paid.</p>
</div>
