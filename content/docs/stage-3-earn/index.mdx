---
title: "Stage 3: Earn"
description: "Test commitment, price, and channel. Earn real commitment before building anything heavy."
---

You now have a real problem and a clear gap. Stage 3 is where you earn proof
that people will commit to your offer, at a price, through a real channel.
Interest is cheap. Commitment changes the game.

## Stage Promise
By the end of Stage 3, you will have:

- An offer that earns commitment
- A price range people accept
- A channel that reaches buyers predictably

If you cannot get commitment, almost nothing else matters.
Keep it simple and real today.

## What This Stage Produces
Stage 3 turns a proven problem into a tested offer. You will build three
artifacts that turn intent into evidence:

- **Offer Test Result** (commitment log + outcomes)
- **Price Range Test** (ladder + buyer reactions)
- **Channel Hypothesis** (tested paths + early signal)

These are the proof points that justify moving into Stage 4.

Your goal is not to be right once. Your goal is to be right enough to bet
the next 90 days on this offer.

## Stage Rules (Keep It Honest)
This stage works only if you follow a few rules:

- Commitment beats compliments. Time or money is the signal.
- Price is a test, not a guess.
- Channels must reach buyers, not just traffic.
- If the commitment is weak, fix the offer before you build.

Speed matters here, but evidence matters more. Earn the signal.

## How the Modules Fit
This stage has three modules, each with a specific activity:

1. **Test if people will commit to the offer** (Activity: Offer Test)  
   You earn a real yes before you build.

2. **Find a price people will actually pay** (Activity: Price Check)  
   You bracket a range and test for real willingness to pay.

3. **Find a simple way to reach buyers** (Activity: Channel Probe)  
   You test access paths until one shows life.

Each module builds evidence for the next. If Module 1 is weak, pricing is
fiction. If Module 2 is unclear, channels are noise. Do them in order.

## What to Ignore for Now
Stage 3 is not the time for:

- Building the full product
- Over‑optimizing pricing models
- Scaling paid acquisition
- Hiring a sales team

Those moves depend on commitment you have not earned yet.

## Common Traps (Avoid Them Early)
Most founders get stuck in one of these:

- Treating “likes” as demand
- Discounting before testing real willingness to pay
- Blaming the channel instead of fixing the offer
- Pitching to friends instead of buyers
- Chasing every channel at once

If you recognize one, stop and reset. The goal is not speed. The goal is
signal.

## Pace and Volume
Speed matters here, but only if you keep the signal clean. A good rhythm is:

- One offer test within two weeks
- One price ladder test with real reactions
- One channel sprint with clear pass signals

Avoid over‑polishing. Your job is to earn commitment, not to perfect a deck.

## Evidence Standard (What Counts)
You do not need scale, but you do need real signals:

- A buyer commits time or money
- A price range survives a real test
- A channel produces qualified conversations

If you do not have those signals, pause and fix the gap.

## Reality Check (Fast)
Ask yourself:

- "Did anyone commit time or money without being pushed?"
- "Did the price test survive real objections?"
- "Can I reach a buyer this week without magic?"

If any answer is no, slow down and fix it. This stage is cheaper than a bad
build.

## Evidence Depth (Strong vs. Weak)
Not all signals are equal. Use this lens:

- **Weak**: likes, interest, “let me know”
- **Medium**: detailed objections, clear use cases, time spent
- **Strong**: deposits, pilots, or repeated commitments

Stage 3 is about moving toward strong signals. If you are stuck in weak
signals, avoid moving on.

## What Success Feels Like
By the end of this stage, you should be able to say, “This offer earns
commitment, this price works, and this channel reaches buyers.” If you cannot,
you may not be ready to build.

Stage 3 is your gate. It protects you from building a product no one buys.

## Handoff to Module 1
Start with the offer. If the offer does not earn commitment, nothing else
matters. Module 1 begins with the Offer Test.

One last reminder: commitment first, always.
