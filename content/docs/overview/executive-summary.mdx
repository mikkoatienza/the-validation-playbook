Most founders do not fail from lack of effort. They fail from the wrong kind of effort: building before proof. This book replaces guesswork with evidence so readers can choose ideas worth their time and energy.

The Validation Playbook is a story-driven guide for early-stage founders who want conviction without hype. It teaches a practical method for finding real pain, testing the riskiest assumptions first, and earning commitment before building anything heavy.

**What you will learn**

- Find real problems even if you do not have a startup idea yet
- Design tests that produce signal, not compliments
- Run customer conversations that surface past behavior
- Diagnose root problems and score them for real potential
- Create offers that earn commitment before a full build
- Decide what to build, pivot, or drop based on evidence
- Install a weekly momentum system that keeps you moving

This book does not promise guaranteed outcomes. It gives you a repeatable way to increase your odds and build around proof instead of hope.

## Who This Is For

- **I have a vague idea** -- You need real validation. Start with [Stage 1: Friction Scan](/docs/stage-1-identify/friction-scan) to test whether the pain is real.
- **I have no idea yet** -- You want a structured way to find one. Start with the [Introduction](/docs) and let the evidence guide you.
- **I am already building** -- You want to audit your assumptions. Jump to [Stage 2: Risk Map](/docs/stage-2-diagnose/risk-map) to find what you may have skipped.
- **I am an operator** -- You want a founder-ready idea to pursue. Read the full playbook from Stage 1 through the [30-Day Proof Plan](/docs/closing/thirty-day-proof-plan).

## The IDEAL Framework

The playbook is built around five stages. Each stage has a distinct purpose, a specific activity, and a gate you must pass before moving on.

| Stage | Name | Question It Answers | Modules |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Identify](/docs/stage-1-identify) | Is the pain real? | Friction Scan, Truth Interviews, Buyer Definition |
| 2 | [Diagnose](/docs/stage-2-diagnose) | Is it worth solving? | Risk Map, Problem Proof, Alternatives Scan |
| 3 | [Earn](/docs/stage-3-earn) | Will anyone pay? | Offer Test, Price Check, Channel Probe |
| 4 | [Assess](/docs/stage-4-assess) | Do they come back? | MVP Sprint, Fit Signals, Fit Fixes |
| 5 | [Lock](/docs/stage-5-lock) | Should I go all in? | Decision Gate, Founder Fit, Momentum OS |

After the five stages, two extensions cover [Positioning and Edge](/docs/extensions/positioning-and-edge) and [Momentum Loops](/docs/extensions/momentum-loops). The playbook ends with a [30-Day Proof Plan](/docs/closing/thirty-day-proof-plan) you can run immediately.

## How Each Stage Works

Every stage follows the same cadence:

1. Read the stage opening to understand the goal and rules.
2. Complete three modules, each with a specific exercise.
3. Review your evidence in the stage closing.
4. Decide whether to advance, tighten, or reset.

Each stage uses a different format to keep the learning fresh -- workshop, myth-bust, story-to-framework, field manual, and letter. You are not asked to do everything at once. You are asked to earn the next stage before you move forward.
