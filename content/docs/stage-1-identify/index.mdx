By the end of Stage 1, you should have **3 validated ICP-problem pairs** with evidence notes. That is the raw material for everything that follows. If you cannot name the buyer and the pain, you are still guessing. This stage is how you stop guessing.

You do not need a perfect idea to begin. You need proof that real pain exists. Stage 1 is where you earn that proof. It moves you from intuition to evidence.

## The Three Modules

Each module builds on the last. If Module 1 is weak, Module 2 turns fuzzy. If Module 2 is vague, Module 3 becomes guesswork. Do them in order.

## Stage Rules

<Callout type="warn" title="Keep it honest">
- Evidence beats opinions. Past behavior beats future promises.
- Avoid pitching. When you pitch, you poison the signal.
- You are allowed to be wrong. You are not allowed to skip the evidence.
- If the pain is weak, narrow the problem or walk away.
</Callout>

Clarity is not a feeling. It is a trail of proof.

<Callout type="error" title="Common traps">
- Falling in love with the first idea
- Treating compliments as proof
- Interviewing people who are not the buyer
- Chasing edge cases instead of repeated pain
- Writing a pitch instead of listening
</Callout>
