---
title: "Stage 1: Identify"
description: "Find real pain worth solving. Earn proof that the problem exists before you build anything."
---

You do not need a perfect idea to begin.  
You need proof that real pain exists.  
Stage 1 is where you earn that proof.

## Stage Promise
By the end of Stage 1, you should have 3 validated ideal customer profile
(ICP)–problem pairs with
evidence notes. That is the raw material for everything that follows.
Keep it small and real.

If you cannot name the buyer and the pain, you are still guessing. That is
normal at the start. This stage is how you stop guessing.

## What This Stage Produces
Stage 1 moves you from intuition to evidence. You will build a small set of
artifacts that make the problem real and testable:

- **Friction Inventory** (20 friction items and top patterns)
- **Customer Truth Notes** (proof of past behavior and spend)
- **ICP Definition (ideal customer profile)** (buyer map, access path, decision context)

These are not essays. They are working tools you will use in Stage 2.

## Stage Rules (Keep It Honest)
This stage works best when you follow a few rules:

- Evidence beats opinions. Past behavior beats future promises.
- Avoid pitching. When you pitch, you poison the signal.
- You are allowed to be wrong. You are not allowed to skip the evidence.
- If the pain is weak, narrow the problem or walk away.

Clarity is not a feeling. It is a trail of proof.

## How the Modules Fit
This stage has three modules, each with a specific activity:

1. **Find a problem people will pay to fix** (Activity: Friction Scan)  
   You collect raw friction and turn it into patterns that repeat.

2. **Get the truth about what people actually do** (Activity: Truth Interviews)  
   You test those patterns against real behavior, not polite interest.

3. **Identify who decides and pays** (Activity: Buyer Definition)  
   You map the buyer, the user, and the decision path so you stop guessing.

Each module builds on the last. If Module 1 is weak, Module 2 turns fuzzy. If
Module 2 is vague, Module 3 becomes guesswork. Do them in order.

## What to Ignore for Now
Stage 1 is not the time for:

- Feature lists or product names
- Technical build choices
- Pricing debates
- Marketing channels
- Fundraising or pitch decks

Those decisions depend on evidence you do not have yet. If you jump ahead,
you will build a solution looking for a problem.

## Common Traps (Avoid Them Early)
Most founders get stuck in one of these:

- Falling in love with the first idea
- Treating compliments as proof
- Interviewing people who are not the buyer
- Chasing edge cases instead of repeated pain
- Writing a pitch instead of listening

If you recognize one, stop and reset. The goal is not speed. The goal is
accuracy.

## Pace and Volume
Speed matters here, but only if you keep the evidence clean. A good rhythm is:

- One friction scan in a single sitting
- 5 to 8 interviews over two weeks
- One buyer map draft, revised after evidence

Do not over‑polish. Your job is to surface the truth, not to perfect the
phrasing.

## Evidence Standard (What Counts)
You do not need perfect data, but you do need real signals:

- A buyer has tried to fix the problem before.
- The pain has a visible cost in time, money, or risk.
- The buyer can be reached this month without magic.

If you do not have those signals, pause and fix the gap.

## What Success Feels Like
By the end of this stage, your idea should feel smaller and sharper, not
bigger and fuzzier. You should be able to explain the pain in one sentence,
name the buyer without hesitation, and show proof that the pain is real.

If you cannot do that yet, you are not behind. You are early. Stage 1 exists
to turn early uncertainty into earned clarity.

## If You Are Starting Without an Idea
That is normal. You will start with observation, not invention. Use the
friction scan to find repeated pain. The idea will emerge from the evidence,
not the other way around.

This stage is your filter. It protects you from building the wrong idea with
the right effort.

## Handoff to Module 1
Start with raw friction. Avoid brainstorming solutions. Open your eyes, collect
pain, and let the patterns speak. Module 1 begins with the Friction Scan.

One last reminder: evidence first, always.
