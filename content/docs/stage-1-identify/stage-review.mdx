title: "Stage 1 Review"
description: "Checklist, readiness gate, and retrospective for Stage 1: Identify."
---

You started this stage with uncertainty. You end it with evidence. That shift
is the real win. You are no longer guessing which problems matter. You have a
shortlist of problems, buyers, and proof you can defend.

## The Three Artifacts That Matter
If you completed Stage 1 as designed, you now have:

- A Friction Inventory with repeated patterns
- Customer Truth Notes grounded in real behavior
- A clear ICP (ideal customer profile) and buyer map you can reach

That is the minimum foundation for Stage 2.

## Stage 1 Completion Checklist (30 Seconds)
Use this compact gate to decide if you should move on:

**Evidence**
- **Friction Scan:** 20 items collected, 5 patterns that repeat across sources
- **Truth Interviews:** 5 to 8 interviews with past actions and spend
- **Buyer Definition:** 3 ICP‑problem pairs with a decision path and access route
- **Evidence of cost:** at least one problem with a visible cost and a workaround

**Artifacts**
- Friction Inventory (top patterns + notes)
- Customer Truth Notes (behavior, spend, workaround)
- ICP Definition (buyer map, access path, decision context)
- One‑sentence buyer thesis: “We help [buyer] solve [pain] because [evidence].”

If any box is empty, or you cannot write the buyer thesis, slow down and fill it.

## If the Evidence Is Thin
Do not force the next stage. Use one of these fixes first:

- Narrow the problem and repeat the scan
- Interview a different buyer segment
- Focus on one workflow and retest
- Drop the weakest pattern and elevate the strongest one

The goal is not to finish fast. The goal is to finish right.

## Module Recap (What Each One Gave You)
Stage 1 worked because each module did a specific job:

- **Friction Scan** gave you raw pain without bias.
- **Truth Interviews** showed what people actually do, not what they say.
- **Buyer Definition** made the decision path visible so you can reach it.

If any module feels weak, fix that weakness before you move on. A weak
foundation will crack under the pressure of Stage 2.

## Stage 1 Retrospective (Two Questions)
Write short answers before you move forward:

- Which problem surprised you with real evidence?
- Which problem looked good but collapsed under truth?

That reflection sharpens your judgment for the next stage.

## Evidence Snapshot (One Paragraph)
Before you move on, write a single paragraph that captures:

- The problem you chose and why it repeats
- The buyer who owns it and how you reach them
- The proof that the pain costs real time, money, or risk

Keep it tight. If you cannot write it in one paragraph, the evidence is still
too fuzzy. Tighten it now while the interviews and notes are fresh.

## Carry‑Forward Signals
Before you enter Stage 2, underline the strongest signals in your notes:

- The single pain that shows up most often
- The buyer segment that responds fastest
- The most convincing piece of proof (behavior, spend, or workaround)

Those signals become your starting assumptions in Diagnose. Keep them visible
all week.

## If You Want to Move Faster
Speed is good, but only if the evidence keeps up. Here is a safe way to move
faster without skipping the truth:

- Collapse the list to one problem, then go deeper, not wider
- Run two extra interviews with the buyer, not the user
- Tighten the buyer map until the decision path is clear

Moving fast means doing less, not doing sloppy. Keep the evidence clean and
the decision will come faster.

## After You Leave This Stage
You will be tempted to jump to solutions the moment you feel clarity. Resist
that urge. Stage 2 is where you stress‑test the problem itself.

Stage 2 will ask a harder question: “Is this pain worth it?” You will build a
risk map, score the problem, and scan alternatives. That work only makes sense
if your Stage 1 evidence is real.

## The Handoff to Stage 2: Diagnose
You are about to move from “Is this pain real?” to “Is this pain worth it?”
If you pass this gate, you are no longer someone exploring ideas without
evidence. You are a founder with evidence.

## Closing Line
If you can name the buyer, the pain, and the proof, you are ready to diagnose.
